# Use Your Thoozle

**A Brief on the Brain, for Anyone Who Has One.**

🧠 **[Read it here →](https://barkbarkbarkbarkbarkbarkbark.github.io/thoozle/)**

---

Your brain does three things: it **detects** what's happening, **predicts** what's about to happen, and **selects an action** based on the difference. That's it. Every thought you've ever had, every feeling you've ever felt, every decision you've ever made — it all comes back to this loop.

> *Detect → Predict → Act*

This loop runs in dogs and birds and fish. The human version is just spectacularly elaborate — we can run it on things that aren't even real. We call that *thinking*.

### A taste

**On motivation:**
> Motivation is not a feeling. It's the output of a calculation. Change the inputs, and the output changes.

**On the Impossible Task:**
> A task becomes impossible when the brain's cost-benefit analysis returns a value that doesn't justify the energy expenditure. It's not laziness. It's arithmetic.

**On wanting vs. liking:**
> "I want to exercise" (you like the idea, the results, the identity) but you don't *want* to exercise (the wanting system isn't generating drive toward the action, because the immediate cost-benefit doesn't favor it).

**On drugs:**
> Drugs don't do anything your brain isn't already doing to itself. They're lock-picks, shaped to fit locks that were already there.

**On being human:**
> The brain is a prediction engine that outgrew its original job description. It was built to keep an animal alive in the present moment, and it ended up capable of simulating entire lives that haven't been lived yet. This is humanity's greatest gift and most persistent source of suffering.
